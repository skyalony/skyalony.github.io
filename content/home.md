## Zhiqi Wang

### Bio

I am a PhD student in labor economics at the School of Labor and Human Resources, Renmin University of China. My main research interests are development economics, labor economics, and trade/spatial economics.

My current work focuses more specifically on state-owned enterprise reform and the farmland redline policy. I may further explore firm dynamics and labor market power, consumption insurance, marriage and fertility, and occupational choice.

### Research Highlights {#highlights}

#### Development Economics

Current projects focus on state-owned enterprise reform and the farmland redline policy, with attention to institutional change and regional development.

#### Labor Economics

I am interested in labor market outcomes, firm dynamics and labor market power, occupational choice, and related household decisions such as marriage and fertility.

#### Trade and Spatial Economics

I am also interested in trade and spatial economics, especially how policies and market frictions shape the allocation of firms, workers, and economic activity across space.
